# Evidence Handling Notes

These are basic notes for handling a suspicious email or link in a careful way.

## What To Preserve

- The original email if possible.
- Sender, recipient, subject, and date.
- Full email headers if they are available.
- URLs exactly as they appeared in the message.
- Screenshots of warnings or suspicious pages, if safe to capture.
- Names and hashes of downloaded files, if a file was downloaded.

## What To Avoid

- Do not click suspicious links just to see what happens.
- Do not enter usernames, passwords, MFA codes, or personal details.
- Do not forward the email widely.
- Do not delete the original message before it is reviewed.
- Do not run downloaded files on your normal computer.

## Simple Investigation Steps

1. Save the message or copy the full text.
2. Extract the links without opening them.
3. Compare the sender domain with the link domains.
4. Look for urgency, account warnings, or payment language.
5. Record findings in a short report.
6. Escalate to IT or security if the risk looks medium or high.

These notes are written for learning. A real workplace investigation should follow the organisation's policies and legal requirements.
