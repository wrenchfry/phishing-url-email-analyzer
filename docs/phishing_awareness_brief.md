# Phishing Awareness Brief

Phishing messages try to make people act quickly before they think. They often pretend to be a support team, a bank, a delivery service, a game service, or a workplace system.

## Common Warning Signs

- The message says your account will be locked or suspended.
- The sender asks you to verify your password or account details.
- The link text looks normal, but the destination domain is different.
- The message uses a shortened link such as `bit.ly` or `tinyurl.com`.
- The URL uses an IP address instead of a normal domain name.
- The message pushes a download you were not expecting.

## Safer Behaviour

- Do not open links from messages that feel urgent or threatening.
- Go to the official website by typing the address yourself.
- Check the sender address and the link domain before clicking.
- Report suspicious messages to IT or security.
- If you already clicked, change passwords from a clean device and revoke active sessions where possible.
